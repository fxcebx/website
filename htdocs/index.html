Hiveeyes in a nutshell

Who are you?

We are a group of beekeepers from Berlin regularly meeting on wednesdays since 2014 to build a collaborative telemetry platform for beehive monitoring. We are Karsten, Robert, Clemens, Richard, Martin, Andreas and Dazz.
Feel welcome to join us.

What is it?

Hiveeyes is an open source, DIY toolkit for building beehive monitoring setups. It is modular and built upon contemporary technologies and components like Arduino, MQTT, InfluxDB and Grafana. With low-cost, low-power hardware, we aim at making non-invasive beekeeping as affordable and comfortable as possible.
The backend systems do not require any 3rd-party services to work. While available as a hosted system running on our shared platform "swarm.hiveeyes.org", it also works self-contained even on a RaspberryPi or similar System-on-A-Chip computer.

How is it going?

After a while of planning and discussions in 2014 and reusing previous efforts of the founding team members, we finally entered the main designing and building phase in 2015/2016.
We are now at a point where we nailed the components of the system and open sourced all major software artifacts required to run the whole stack, from embedded firmware to backend systems. The source code is on GitHub, so we are happy to share our efforts completely with the community and look forward to the next development iterations, now completely in the open.

And now?

Beginning autumn 2016, we will reiterate on different aspects of the software- and hardware-components, decide about which hardware route to follow and hopefully will start producing some small batches.
We will also put some efforts into a scientific project we were asked to collaborate with and always look forward to your feedback. There are many bits and pieces to look at and require more intensive testing. We need you!
